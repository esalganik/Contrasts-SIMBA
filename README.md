Snow depth and sea ice thickness from SIMBA buoys 2025T143, 2025T144, 2025T145, 2025T135, and 2025T136 during the CONTRASTS expedition
(Evgenii Salganik, Dmitry Divine, Ran Tao, Marcel Nicolaus, 2026)

Temperature and heating measurements were obtained from five SIMBA (Snow and Ice Mass Balance Array) buoys deployed on Arctic sea ice during the CONTRASTS expedition in 2025. The dataset includes observations from buoys 2025T143, 2025T144, 2025T145, 2025T135, and 2025T136, with processed data coverage between 12 July and 15 November 2025.

The buoys recorded vertical temperature profiles in snow and sea ice and temperature changes during heating cycles (30 s and 120 s). The dataset contains time, latitude, longitude, sensor depth relative to the water level, temperature, and heating-derived temperature change.

Positions of the air-snow, snow-ice, and ice-water interfaces were derived from the temperature profiles and supplemented by manual measurements. Snow thickness and sea ice thickness were calculated from the interface positions.
